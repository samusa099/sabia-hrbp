# Project Story and Problem Declaration

## 1. Why the HRBP was hired
Sabia Group had already committed large capital to smartwatch manufacturing. Instead of validating an MVP and building a prototype, the business went directly into full production. Bangladesh-based precision manufacturing skills were scarce, recruitment was broad rather than capability-led, and process ownership was unclear.

The result:
- low first-pass yield;
- high defect, rework and warranty exposure;
- excessive overtime;
- duplicated and mismatched roles;
- inconsistent people data;
- recurring operating loss;
- an active discussion about selling the business.

The Head of HR proposed a strategic HRBP appointment. The selected person performs a dual role: **Lead HRBP and Head of HR / Acting CHRO**.

## 2. Mandate
The role is not a routine HR operations role. It is accountable for:
- identifying where people, organization and capability are damaging business performance;
- designing a defensible workforce reset;
- hiring scarce technical skills;
- building certification and performance systems;
- linking HR metrics with production and financial results;
- running a controlled test before large-scale rollout.

## 3. The difficult Q1 decision
The company began with 100 employees. Q1 diagnostics indicated that the existing structure was unaffordable and did not match the capability needed for precision smartwatch production. A 48-role workforce reset was therefore modelled and implemented.

This was not treated as a simple cost-cutting exercise. The project dataset includes:
- documented business reasons;
- skill and role criteria;
- protected-attribute exclusion;
- legal and ethical review;
- separation benefits;
- knowledge-transfer risk;
- rehire eligibility.

## 4. Quarter-by-quarter logic
### Q1 — Feasibility and workforce reset
Baseline the business, define KPIs, compare scenarios, redesign work and receive permission for a limited pilot.

### Q2 — Controlled pilot
Test the operating model with 25 employees. Compare pilot and control outcomes. Scale only after evidence.

### Q3 — Scale and stabilize
Expand to two lines, hire critical talent, build dashboards, introduce recognition and run weekly HR-business reviews.

### Q4 — Enterprise rollout
Extend the model across Sabia Group, integrate HR-production-finance reporting and obtain full approval.

## 5. Business-partner outcome
The project demonstrates how HR can move from administrative reporting to business recovery by connecting:
`skills → behaviour → process quality → output → cost → profitability`.
